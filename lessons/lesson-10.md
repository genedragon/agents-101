# Lesson 10: Showcase & Launchpad

**Duration:** 60–90 minutes
**Tools used:** Everything (students present); browser for the launchpad
**Installs required:** None
**Cost:** $0

---

## Learning objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to:

1. **Present something they built** in ~2 minutes — what it is, who it's for, how they made it with an agent
2. **Reflect on the perceive → decide → act journey** from week 1 to now
3. **Leave with a concrete "keep going" plan** — the single most important outcome of the club
4. **Know where to continue** — communities, free resources, next-project ideas

---

## What students will produce

- A 2-minute showcase of their favorite project (live URL on the screen)
- A completed **"My Launchpad"** plan: their next project + where they'll keep learning
- (Optional) a spot in the public "Class of 2026" gallery linking all their portfolios

---

## Why this lesson matters

This is the payoff for the **TOP GOAL**: students who want to keep learning walk out with the tools *and a plan* to continue on their own. The showcase builds pride and cements identity ("I'm someone who builds things"). The launchpad turns a finished club into an ongoing journey — which was the entire point.

---

## Timing plan (75-minute session — adjust ±15 min)

| Time | Block | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 – 0:05 | Welcome | Set the celebratory tone; how showcases run |
| 0:05 – 0:45 | Showcases | Each student presents ~2 min (scale to cohort size) |
| 0:45 – 0:58 | Launchpad | Everyone fills in their "keep going" plan |
| 0:58 – 1:10 | Resources | Where to keep learning (below) |
| 1:10 – 1:15 | Send-off | Certificates / gallery link / final word |

---

## Instructor notes

### Welcome (5 min)

> "Ten weeks ago most of you had never written a line of code or heard the word 'agent.' Today you'll show something you built and put on the internet. This is a celebration. Be generous with applause."

### Showcases (40 min — scale to cohort)

Each student gets ~2 minutes on the projector with their live site up:
1. What it is + who it's for
2. A quick demo (click the thing)
3. One thing they're proud of + one thing an agent got wrong that they fixed

**Instructor + TA:** applaud every single one. Ask one warm question each. No critique today — pure celebration. (For larger cohorts, run two rooms or lightning-round to 90 seconds.)

### Launchpad (13 min)

Everyone fills in the **"My Launchpad"** sheet (Assignment 10):
- My next project idea
- One community I'll join
- One resource I'll use
- One thing I want to learn next

The point: nobody leaves without a *next step*.

### Resources — where to keep learning (12 min)

Put these on the screen (all free):

**Keep building**
- Their Kiro free tier (50 credits/mo, perpetual) — it doesn't expire when the club ends
- GitHub + GitHub Pages — keep publishing to the same portfolio
- Their KiroClaw/OpenClaw setup — keep the scheduled jobs running

**Keep learning (free)**
- freeCodeCamp — free, beginner-friendly, project-based
- The Odin Project — free full web-dev curriculum
- GitHub Education pack — free tools for verified students
- Official docs for the tools they used (Kiro, GitHub Pages)
- YouTube: search the exact thing they want to build

**Communities**
- The club's ongoing chat (keep it alive after the course)
- Beginner-friendly forums / Discords the instructor vets
- Local hackathons and school CS club

**Habits that matter more than any tool**
- Build small things often
- Read what the agent writes — stay in control
- Be honest about what's AI-made
- When stuck, write a good bug report and ask

### Send-off (5 min)

> "You don't need this club to keep going — that was the whole point. You have the tools, a portfolio, and a plan. Go build things. Help your family and friends use this stuff well. And stay honest about what you make and how. Go."

(Optional: certificates, the public "Class of 2026" gallery link, a group photo.)

## Common pitfalls (instructor cheatsheet)

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Student's project isn't "done" | Normal | Celebrate progress, not polish — "done enough" was always the bar |
| Nervous to present | Stage fright | Offer to co-present with the TA; keep it low-key and warm |
| "What do I do now that it's over?" | The exact question to answer | That's what the Launchpad is for — make sure theirs is filled in |
| Wants to keep going but worried about cost | Cost anxiety | Reassure: Kiro free tier + GitHub + hosting are all $0 and perpetual |
| Finished early / bored | Fast finisher | Have them help a peer present, or start their next-project sketch |

## Materials needed

- Projector that can quickly switch between students' live URLs
- The "My Launchpad" sheet (Assignment 10) printed or shared
- The resources list on a slide
- (Optional) certificates, a "Class of 2026" gallery page, a camera
- Snacks help. This is a party.

---

## Homework — there isn't any. But...

- Actually start your next project within two weeks — momentum fades fast
- Join the one community you picked
- Keep your portfolio live and add to it

---

## Optional stretch (for anyone who wants to go further)

> "Teach someone else one thing you learned in this club — a parent, a sibling, a friend. Teaching it is how you really own it. That was one of our goals from day one."

## What's next

There's no lesson 11 — that's the point. Students have the tools, a live portfolio, an agent that remembers them, and a plan. The course succeeds if they keep building without it.
