What I learned

  • An agent doesn't just answer — it does things: reads files, writes code, runs it, then checks its own work.
  • It's fast, but it's not always right. Reading what it wrote and catching mistakes is the actual skill.
  • Saying exactly what I want gets a way better result than being vague. Prompts are just clear thinking.
  • Publishing something to the real internet is less scary than I thought. This page is proof.
  • I can keep going after this club ends. That was the whole point.

Thanks for looking. More coming — I'm not done.
