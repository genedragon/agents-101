# Assignment 1: Three Agents in My Life

**Due:** End of session 1 (start in class, finish at home if needed)
**Estimated time:** 20–30 minutes
**Format:** Submit as paper, Google Doc, or Notes app entry — student's choice. We'll go fully digital starting week 6.

---

## Goal

Show that you can spot an agent in the wild and explain why it counts as agentic — using the **perceive → decide → act** framework from class.

---

## Part 1 — Your own definition (5 min)

In one sentence, in your own words, finish this:

> An agent is...

Don't quote the instructor. Don't quote a website. Write what you actually understand.

---

## Part 2 — Three agents in your life (15 min)

Pick three things you use that you think are agents. Examples to spark ideas (but please pick your own):

- Voice assistants (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant)
- Smart home stuff (Roomba, smart thermostat, security camera)
- A self-driving car or driver-assist feature
- An AI feature in a game you play
- An AI tool in an app you use (TikTok recommendations? Spotify? YouTube?)
- Anti-spam in your email
- A chatbot on a customer-service website that actually *did* something for you

For **each** of your three picks, fill this in:

| Field | Example answer |
|---|---|
| **What it is** | "Alexa setting a timer when I ask" |
| **What it perceives** | "It hears my voice and converts it to text" |
| **What it decides** | "It figures out 'set timer for 5 minutes' is a command and what value the timer needs" |
| **What it acts on** | "It starts a timer in the Alexa app and confirms with a beep" |
| **Is this fully an agent, partly an agent, or just a chatbot? Why?** | "Fully an agent — it acted on the world (started a timer) without me doing anything else" |

---

## Part 3 — One thing that looks like an agent but isn't (5 min)

Pick one thing that *seems* like it should be an agent, but really isn't (it's just a chatbot, calculator, or recommendation system).

Explain why it doesn't count.

> Example: "Google's search suggestions seem agentic, but they're just predictions. The search engine doesn't go do anything until I click. So it's more like autocomplete than an agent."

---

## Part 4 — Stretch question (optional)

> Can a tool be "kind of" an agent? Pick one of your examples from Part 2 and argue *against* it being a real agent. (Even if you disagree with yourself.)

---

## How this gets graded

This is a "did you try?" assignment, not a right-or-wrong one. The instructor and TA will read every one and write a short comment back. We're looking for:

- You actually filled in the perceive / decide / act fields with specifics
- You picked your own examples (not just the ones from class)
- Your reasoning shows you can tell agents apart from chatbots and recommendation systems
