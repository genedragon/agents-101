# Assignment 9: An Agent That Works While You Sleep (Advanced)

**Due:** End of session 9 (finish at home if needed)
**Estimated time:** 20–30 minutes
**Format:** KiroClaw (or OpenClaw). Notebook for reflections.

> Advanced track. The payoff: something your agent does on its own, on a schedule.

---

## Goal

Set up one scheduled job (cron) that runs on its own and produces a real result.

---

## Part 1 — Set up a scheduled job (15 min)

Pick one safe, useful job and set it up:

- A daily "good morning" note with today's date + a fun fact
- A daily study reminder at a set time
- A weekly nudge that reads your project plan and reminds you what to build next

Write down: what job did you set, and for what schedule?

---

## Part 2 — See it run (10 min)

Trigger it now (or set it ~2 minutes out) so you can watch it fire **without you asking in the moment**. Confirm the output landed.

Write one line: *what did it produce, and did anything feel different about it happening on its own?*

---

## Part 3 — Delegate to a subagent (thought exercise) (5 min)

Describe one bigger task you'd split across helper agents (subagents). What would each helper do?

> Example: "Research three colleges — one subagent per college — then combine their findings."

---

## Part 4 — Responsibility (5 min)

Answer in 2–3 sentences: name one job you'd trust an agent to do **unwatched**, and one you **wouldn't**. What's the difference?

---

## Part 5 — Stretch (optional)

Teach your agent a lesson via self-learning ("always keep summaries under 3 sentences"), then check in a later session whether it stuck. Note the result.

---

## How this gets graded

"Did you try?" We're looking for:
- One scheduled job that actually ran
- A thoughtful subagent idea
- A real answer to the "watched vs unwatched" responsibility question

---

## Bring next week — SHOWCASE!

- Your **favorite project**, ready to show in 2 minutes (live URL)
- Your portfolio updated so your best work is front-and-center
- Any scheduled-job output you're proud of

---

## If you're stuck

Scheduling syntax is the tricky part — copy the example from class and change the time. If the job fires but does nothing, make the task more specific and check whether it needed a permission. Keep schedules to daily/weekly (never every minute) so you don't spam yourself. Ask in the club chat.
